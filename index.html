Major Milestones

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore found Intel.

Intel begins in Silicon Valley and grows from memory into processors and platforms.

1970

1103 DRAM Breakthrough

A pivotal memory product helps put Intel on the map.

Intel’s early success came from memory technology before CPUs became the center of the business.

1971

4004 Microprocessor

Intel introduces the 4004, a landmark microprocessor.

Often credited as a key step toward the modern digital age by putting computing on a chip.

1971

Intel IPO

Intel goes public.

Becoming publicly traded helps fund growth during the early semiconductor boom.

1974

8080 Processor

A major early CPU used in many early systems.

The 8080 is widely recognized as a foundational chip in early microcomputer history.

1978

8086: x86 Begins

Intel introduces the 8086, launching the x86 era.

x86 architecture becomes the long-running foundation for many PCs and servers.

1981

IBM PC Uses Intel

The IBM PC era accelerates the Intel CPU standard.

Major platform adoption helps x86 become a dominant computing standard.

1985

386 Processor

A key step forward in PC performance and capability.

The 386 generation becomes an important chapter in Intel’s rise in PC computing.

1989

486 Processor

Intel advances CPU performance through the late 80s/early 90s.

The 486 era is part of the momentum that leads into the Pentium brand years.

1991

“Intel Inside” Campaign

Intel branding becomes consumer-facing.

A major marketing move that tied Intel CPUs to the identity of the PC.

1993

Pentium Launch

Pentium becomes one of the best-known CPU brands ever.

The brand era helps define 90s PC growth and Intel dominance.

1999

Wi-Fi Adoption & Mobility

Intel pushes into mobile computing trends.

This era sets the stage for laptop-centered computing growth in the 2000s.

2006

Intel Core Era

Core branding becomes central to consumer CPUs.

Core processors become a long-running platform for laptops and desktops.

2011

Data Center Momentum

Server and data center chips grow in importance.

Intel’s server strategy becomes a major pillar of the business for years.

2012

3D Tri-Gate Transistors

Intel highlights a major transistor architecture shift in manufacturing.

Intel’s chip-history materials point to Tri-Gate as a signature manufacturing innovation.

2021

IDM 2.0 + Foundry Push

Intel announces IDM 2.0 and Intel Foundry Services plans.

Includes major manufacturing expansion plans and a push to serve external foundry customers.

2022

Intel Arc Launch

Intel launches Arc discrete graphics for laptops.

Arc marks Intel’s push into discrete GPU products beyond integrated graphics.

2025

Industry Pressure & Turnaround Focus

Intel’s challenges and strategy shifts remain a major storyline.

Major coverage highlights competition, manufacturing execution, and the AI era as key pressures.
