Major Milestones

Use arrows or let it advance automatically

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore establish Intel.

Founded in Silicon Valley, Intel begins as a memory company.

1970

1103 DRAM

Intel’s first major commercial success.

The 1103 DRAM chip helps establish Intel in the semiconductor market.

1971

4004 Microprocessor

The first commercial microprocessor.

This breakthrough puts an entire CPU onto a single chip.

1971

Intel IPO

Intel becomes a public company.

Going public enables faster growth and innovation.

1974

8080 Processor

Powering early computers.

The 8080 becomes a foundation of early microcomputer systems.

1978

8086 & x86

Birth of the x86 architecture.

x86 becomes the dominant PC processor architecture.

1981

IBM PC Era

Intel CPUs power IBM PCs.

This partnership accelerates Intel’s dominance in computing.

1985

386 Processor

Major leap in performance.

The 386 brings 32-bit computing to mainstream PCs.

1989

486 Processor

Integrated math coprocessor.

Boosts performance and efficiency for early PCs.

1991

Intel Inside

A legendary marketing campaign.

Intel becomes a consumer-recognized brand.

1993

Pentium Launch

A defining CPU brand.

Pentium dominates PCs throughout the 1990s.

1999

Mobile Computing

Intel shifts toward laptops.

Wireless and mobility become core priorities.

2006

Intel Core Era

New generation of CPUs.

Core processors become the backbone of modern PCs.

2012

Tri-Gate Transistors

3D transistor innovation.

A major manufacturing breakthrough for efficiency.

2021

IDM 2.0

Foundry strategy announced.

Intel expands manufacturing and external foundry services.

2022

Intel Arc GPUs

Entry into discrete graphics.

Arc marks Intel’s push into high-performance graphics.

2025

Modern Challenges

AI era and industry competition.

Intel focuses on execution, AI, and manufacturing leadership.
